Type 2 Diabetes Treatment Protocol
=====================================

1. DIAGNOSTIC CRITERIA

Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus is diagnosed when any of the following criteria are met:
- Fasting plasma glucose (FPG) >= 126 mg/dL (7.0 mmol/L)
- 2-hour plasma glucose >= 200 mg/dL during 75g OGTT
- HbA1c >= 6.5% (48 mmol/mol)
- Random plasma glucose >= 200 mg/dL with classic symptoms

Pre-diabetes (increased risk):
- Impaired fasting glucose: FPG 100-125 mg/dL
- Impaired glucose tolerance: 2h glucose 140-199 mg/dL
- HbA1c 5.7-6.4%

2. TREATMENT TARGETS

- HbA1c: <7.0% (general); <6.5% (newly diagnosed, no cardiovascular disease)
- Fasting glucose: 80-130 mg/dL
- Post-prandial glucose (2h): <180 mg/dL
- Blood pressure: <130/80 mmHg
- LDL cholesterol: <100 mg/dL (<70 mg/dL if high CV risk)

3. FIRST-LINE THERAPY: METFORMIN

Metformin HCl:
- Starting dose: 500mg once daily with meals
- Titrate: Increase by 500mg weekly
- Target dose: 1000mg twice daily (max 2550mg/day)
- Mechanism: Reduces hepatic glucose production, increases insulin sensitivity
- Benefits: Weight neutral/slight loss, CV benefit, low hypoglycemia risk
- Side effects: GI upset (nausea, diarrhea), vitamin B12 deficiency
- Contraindications: eGFR <30 mL/min, acute kidney injury, metabolic acidosis
- Monitor: Renal function every 3-6 months, B12 levels annually

4. SECOND-LINE AGENTS

When metformin alone insufficient (HbA1c >7% after 3 months):

A) GLP-1 Receptor Agonists (preferred if CV disease or obesity):
- Semaglutide (Ozempic): 0.25mg SC weekly x4 weeks, then 0.5mg, max 2mg
- Liraglutide (Victoza): 0.6mg SC daily x1 week, then 1.2mg, max 1.8mg
- Dulaglutide (Trulicity): 0.75mg SC weekly, max 4.5mg
Benefits: Weight loss (5-15%), CV risk reduction, renal protection
Side effects: Nausea, vomiting, pancreatitis risk

B) SGLT2 Inhibitors (preferred if heart failure or CKD):
- Empagliflozin (Jardiance): 10mg daily, max 25mg
- Dapagliflozin (Farxiga): 5mg daily, max 10mg
- Canagliflozin (Invokana): 100mg daily, max 300mg
Benefits: Weight loss, BP reduction, CV and renal benefits
Side effects: Genital infections, UTI, DKA risk, volume depletion

C) DPP-4 Inhibitors (if cost or injection concerns):
- Sitagliptin (Januvia): 100mg daily
- Linagliptin (Tradjenta): 5mg daily
- Saxagliptin (Onglyza): 5mg daily
Benefits: Weight neutral, well-tolerated, no hypoglycemia
Side effects: Nasopharyngitis, headache, joint pain

5. INSULIN THERAPY

Initiate when oral agents and GLP-1 RA fail to achieve target:

Basal insulin (start):
- Insulin Glargine (Lantus/Basaglar): 10 units at bedtime, titrate by 2 units q3 days
- Insulin Degludec (Tresiba): 10 units daily, titrate by 2 units q3 days

Basal-Bolus (if basal alone insufficient):
- Add rapid-acting insulin before largest meal
- Insulin Lispro (Humalog): 4 units before meals, adjust by 1-2 units
- Insulin Aspart (NovoRapid): Same dosing as lispro

6. HYPOGLYCEMIA MANAGEMENT

Recognition: Blood glucose <70 mg/dL with symptoms (shakiness, sweating, confusion)
Treatment (Rule of 15):
- Consume 15g fast-acting carbohydrate (glucose tablets, juice)
- Recheck glucose after 15 minutes
- Repeat if still <70 mg/dL
- Follow with complex carbohydrate snack

Severe hypoglycemia (<54 mg/dL): Glucagon 1mg IM/SC or nasal glucagon

7. ANNUAL SCREENING CHECKLIST

- HbA1c: Every 3 months (every 6 months if stable)
- Lipid panel: Annually
- Renal function (eGFR, urine albumin): Annually
- Dilated eye exam: Annually
- Foot exam: Every visit (comprehensive annually)
- Dental exam: Every 6 months
- Influenza vaccine: Annually
- Pneumococcal vaccine: Per age guidelines
- Depression screening: Annually
