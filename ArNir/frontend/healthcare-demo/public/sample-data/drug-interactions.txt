Common Drug Interaction Reference
===================================

This reference covers clinically significant drug interactions frequently encountered in primary care and hospital settings.

1. WARFARIN INTERACTIONS

Warfarin (Coumadin) has extensive drug interactions due to CYP2C9 and CYP3A4 metabolism:

Increased bleeding risk (potentiate warfarin):
- NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen): Increase INR and GI bleeding risk. AVOID concurrent use.
- Aspirin: Additive bleeding risk. Use only if clearly indicated (ACS, mechanical valve).
- Acetaminophen (>2g/day): Moderate INR increase. Limit to <2g/day.
- Amiodarone: Major interaction. Reduce warfarin dose by 30-50%. Monitor INR weekly x4.
- Fluconazole/Ketoconazole: Inhibit CYP2C9. Significant INR increase. Reduce warfarin dose.
- Metronidazole: Inhibits warfarin metabolism. Monitor INR closely.
- SSRIs (fluoxetine, sertraline): Inhibit platelet function and CYP2C9. Monitor INR.

Decreased warfarin effect:
- Vitamin K (green leafy vegetables): Maintain consistent dietary intake.
- Rifampin: Potent CYP inducer. Dramatically reduces warfarin effect. Avoid if possible.
- Carbamazepine: Induces CYP enzymes. Monitor INR, adjust dose.
- St. John's Wort: Induces CYP3A4. Avoid concurrent use.

2. STATIN INTERACTIONS

HMG-CoA reductase inhibitors (atorvastatin, simvastatin, rosuvastatin):

Increased myopathy/rhabdomyolysis risk:
- Grapefruit juice (>1 quart/day): Inhibits CYP3A4. Affects atorvastatin, simvastatin most.
- Gemfibrozil: Major interaction with all statins (especially simvastatin). Use fenofibrate instead.
- Cyclosporine: Major interaction. Use lowest statin dose or pravastatin (less interaction).
- Macrolide antibiotics (erythromycin, clarithromycin): Inhibit CYP3A4. Use azithromycin instead.
- Protease inhibitors (ritonavir): Major CYP3A4 inhibition. Use rosuvastatin or pravastatin.
- Calcium channel blockers (diltiazem, verapamil): Moderate CYP3A4 inhibition.

Safe combinations:
- Pravastatin/Rosuvastatin: Less affected by CYP3A4 inhibitors.
- Ezetimibe: Safe to combine with any statin.

3. SSRI/SNRI INTERACTIONS

Serotonin syndrome risk (potentially fatal):
- SSRIs + Triptans (sumatriptan, rizatriptan): Moderate risk. Use with caution. Educate patient on symptoms.
- SSRIs + Tramadol: Serotonin syndrome risk. Avoid if possible.
- SSRIs + MAOIs: CONTRAINDICATED. Allow 14-day washout between switches (5 weeks for fluoxetine).
- SSRIs + Linezolid: MAOI-like activity. Avoid combination.
- SSRIs + Lithium: Monitor for serotonin syndrome signs.
- SSRIs + Dextromethorphan: Moderate serotonin syndrome risk. Avoid high doses.

Serotonin syndrome symptoms: Agitation, confusion, rapid heart rate, high BP, dilated pupils, muscle twitching, diarrhea, hyperthermia. EMERGENCY if severe.

4. ACE INHIBITOR / ARB INTERACTIONS

Hyperkalemia risk:
- ACE/ARB + Potassium supplements: Monitor K+ levels closely. Adjust supplement dose.
- ACE/ARB + Spironolactone/Eplerenone: Significant hyperkalemia risk. Monitor K+ q1-2 weeks initially.
- ACE/ARB + Trimethoprim: Increases potassium. Monitor levels.
- ACE/ARB + NSAIDs: Triple whammy (+ diuretic) = acute kidney injury risk. Avoid triple combination.

Renal function impact:
- ACE/ARB + NSAIDs: Reduced GFR, fluid retention. Monitor renal function.
- ACE/ARB + Metformin: Monitor renal function. Adjust metformin if eGFR drops.

NEVER combine: ACE inhibitor + ARB (dual RAAS blockade). No benefit, increased AKI and hyperkalemia.

5. METFORMIN INTERACTIONS

Lactic acidosis risk:
- Metformin + IV Contrast dye: Hold metformin 48h before and after contrast. Restart after confirming normal renal function.
- Metformin + Alcohol (excessive): Increased lactic acidosis risk. Moderate alcohol intake only.
- Metformin + Topiramate: Increased metabolic acidosis risk. Monitor bicarbonate.

Reduced absorption:
- Metformin + Antacids (calcium carbonate): Slightly reduced absorption. Space by 2 hours.

6. OPIOID INTERACTIONS

Respiratory depression risk:
- Opioids + Benzodiazepines: BLACK BOX WARNING. Profound sedation, respiratory depression, death. Avoid combination.
- Opioids + Gabapentin/Pregabalin: Increased CNS depression. FDA warning.
- Opioids + Muscle relaxants (cyclobenzaprine): Additive sedation.
- Opioids + Alcohol: Enhanced respiratory depression. Absolute contraindication.

CYP2D6 interactions (codeine, tramadol):
- CYP2D6 inhibitors (fluoxetine, paroxetine, bupropion): Reduce conversion of codeine to morphine. Decreased efficacy.
- CYP2D6 ultra-rapid metabolizers: Increased toxicity with codeine. Avoid codeine in these patients.

7. ANTIBIOTIC INTERACTIONS

Key interactions:
- Ciprofloxacin + Theophylline: Increases theophylline levels. Monitor levels, reduce dose.
- Ciprofloxacin + Antacids/Iron/Calcium: Chelation reduces absorption. Space by 2+ hours.
- Metronidazole + Alcohol: Disulfiram-like reaction (nausea, vomiting, flushing). Avoid alcohol during and 48h after.
- Tetracycline + Dairy/Antacids: Chelation reduces absorption. Take on empty stomach.
- Rifampin + Oral contraceptives: Induces metabolism. Use backup contraception.
