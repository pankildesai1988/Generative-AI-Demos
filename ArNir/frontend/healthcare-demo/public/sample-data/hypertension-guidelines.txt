WHO Hypertension Management Guidelines 2024
=============================================

1. DEFINITION AND CLASSIFICATION

Hypertension is defined as systolic blood pressure (SBP) >= 140 mmHg and/or diastolic blood pressure (DBP) >= 90 mmHg, measured on two separate occasions. Classification:

- Normal: SBP < 120 and DBP < 80 mmHg
- Elevated: SBP 120-129 and DBP < 80 mmHg
- Stage 1 Hypertension: SBP 130-139 or DBP 80-89 mmHg
- Stage 2 Hypertension: SBP >= 140 or DBP >= 90 mmHg
- Hypertensive Crisis: SBP > 180 and/or DBP > 120 mmHg

2. RISK FACTORS

Major modifiable risk factors include:
- High sodium intake (>2g/day)
- Physical inactivity (<150 min/week moderate exercise)
- Obesity (BMI >= 30)
- Excessive alcohol consumption (>2 drinks/day men, >1 drink/day women)
- Tobacco use
- High stress levels
- Poor sleep quality (<6 hours/night)

Non-modifiable risk factors:
- Age (>55 years men, >65 years women)
- Family history of hypertension
- African descent (higher prevalence)
- Chronic kidney disease

3. FIRST-LINE MEDICATIONS

Recommended first-line antihypertensive agents:

A) ACE Inhibitors (ACEi):
- Lisinopril: Starting dose 10mg daily, max 40mg
- Enalapril: Starting dose 5mg daily, max 40mg
- Ramipril: Starting dose 2.5mg daily, max 10mg
Side effects: Dry cough (10-15%), angioedema (rare), hyperkalemia
Contraindications: Pregnancy, bilateral renal artery stenosis

B) Angiotensin Receptor Blockers (ARBs):
- Losartan: Starting dose 50mg daily, max 100mg
- Valsartan: Starting dose 80mg daily, max 320mg
- Telmisartan: Starting dose 40mg daily, max 80mg
Side effects: Dizziness, hyperkalemia (less cough than ACEi)
Preferred when: ACEi-intolerant (cough)

C) Calcium Channel Blockers (CCBs):
- Amlodipine: Starting dose 5mg daily, max 10mg
- Nifedipine XL: Starting dose 30mg daily, max 90mg
Side effects: Peripheral edema, headache, flushing
Preferred when: Elderly patients, isolated systolic hypertension

D) Thiazide Diuretics:
- Hydrochlorothiazide: 12.5-25mg daily
- Chlorthalidone: 12.5-25mg daily (preferred, longer acting)
- Indapamide: 1.25-2.5mg daily
Side effects: Hypokalemia, hyperuricemia, hyperglycemia

4. TREATMENT TARGETS

- General population: <130/80 mmHg
- Elderly (>65 years): <140/90 mmHg
- Diabetes mellitus: <130/80 mmHg
- Chronic kidney disease: <130/80 mmHg
- Post-stroke: <130/80 mmHg

5. LIFESTYLE MODIFICATIONS

All patients should implement lifestyle changes:
- DASH diet (rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, low-fat dairy)
- Sodium restriction to <2g/day
- Regular aerobic exercise: 150 min/week moderate or 75 min/week vigorous
- Weight management: target BMI 18.5-24.9
- Limit alcohol: <=2 standard drinks/day (men), <=1 (women)
- Smoking cessation
- Stress management techniques

6. MONITORING SCHEDULE

- Newly diagnosed: Follow-up at 1 month, then every 3 months until controlled
- Controlled hypertension: Every 6 months
- At each visit: BP measurement, medication review, side effect assessment
- Annual: Renal function (eGFR, electrolytes), fasting glucose, lipid panel
- Home BP monitoring recommended: twice daily (morning and evening)
